# Pricing Ladder Psychology

**Rule:**

1. Middle-tier subscription must include **one proprietary IP item** (not just more leads/units) so it feels like a membership, not a bulk discount.
2. Premium tier must win on **intangibles** — proprietary playbooks, 1:1 access, white-glove service — **not on volume**. Adding more units at the top dilutes the per-unit anchor and trains customers to compare on price.

**Why:** Sam's Pro tier at $397/mo with "2 markets" was indistinguishable from buying 2 Weekly subs at $147 ($294) — a $103 premium for nothing differentiated. Discovered 2026-05-08 during The Friday Pack pricing recalibration. Customers do the per-unit math; if the top tier is just "more of the same," it loses.

**How to apply:**

- Any tier ladder I help Sam design must have a clear **intangibles axis** at the top tier (1:1 access, proprietary IP, custom service) **separate** from the **volume axis**.
- Middle tier: bundle ONE proprietary asset (PDF playbook, template, etc.) so the upgrade story is "you're now a member" not "you bought in bulk."
- Top tier: add concierge / Slack / monthly call / proprietary deck — never just "+1 market" or "+100 units." Volume add-ons live as à la carte upsells (e.g., Friday Pack's +1 market = $97/mo add-on).
- If the per-unit price at the top tier is lower than the tier below it, that is a red flag — re-anchor.
