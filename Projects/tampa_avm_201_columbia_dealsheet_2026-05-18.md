### Verdict
At $2,550 list, this **fails the strict 2x rule** for STR resale.
At ~$1,970 effective (utilities + furniture saved), it **passes a 1.5x rule** which is acceptable for a premium-location pilot.
After negotiation to $2,200-2,300, the math works as a corporate-housing-only play.

## Questions to ask her on the call (in order)

1. "Is the unit still available?" (always)
2. "How long has it been on the market?" — she'll say 5+ days, confirms there's negotiation room
3. "How did your last tenant find you, and how long did they stay?" — gauges turnover history
4. "Are you running it furnished by choice, or did the previous tenant leave the furniture?" — tells you if she's already in the corporate-housing mindset
5. "Would you consider a 24-month corporate housing lease with EIN guarantor + personal guarantee?" — the pitch
6. "Would there be value in me using Maid for You for turnover cleanings?" — the partnership hook
7. "What's the absolute floor you'd take on a 24-month signed today?" — surfaces real walkaway

## Risk flags to ask about (Reality Checker surfaced these)

- BBB / WhoseYourLandlord reviews mention **maintenance complaint** patterns. Ask:
  - "What's your typical repair turnaround on AC, plumbing, appliances?"
  - "Do you handle maintenance yourself or contract it?"
  - **If she's slow on maintenance, that hurts STR reviews badly.** Negotiate either a faster SLA or a maintenance carve-out (Sam handles small repairs under $250).
