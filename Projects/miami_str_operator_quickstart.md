# Miami STR Operator Quickstart

*A turnkey playbook for the Friday Pack: 50 Miami landlord leads → signed corporate master leases.*

---

## 1. The Cold Call — 4 Openers by Landlord Archetype

Every opener follows the same skeleton: **label → value frame → disqualifier.** Labels are Chris Voss tactical empathy — they name the landlord's likely concern *before* they raise it. The disqualifier protects your time; if they fail it, you exit warmly in under 90 seconds.

### a) Multi-Property LLC Owner (Portfolio Play)

> "Hey [Name] — appreciate you picking up. It seems like you've built a real portfolio over the years, and probably the last thing you need is another tenant headache. I run a corporate housing operation placing traveling medical and executive professionals into mid-term furnished units across Miami — we sign 12-month master leases, pay deposit per door, and handle one point of contact across your whole portfolio. Before I take any more of your time — are you currently open to a single-tenant relationship across multiple units, or are you firm on individual leasing only?"

### b) Single-Listing Absentee Owner (Out-of-State)

> "Hi [Name] — sounds like you might be managing this one from out of state, which is usually a grind. I operate corporate furnished housing in Miami and we lease units on 12-month terms, pay first/last/deposit upfront, and our tenants are vetted traveling professionals — nurses, consultants, relocations. Quick one before I waste your time: is the unit currently vacant or coming up for renewal in the next 60 days?"

### c) Older Walkable Building (Hyde Park / Wynwood / Little Havana)

> "Hey [Name] — figured these older walkable buildings probably get a lot of short-term inquiries, and most of them are nightmares for you. I'm different — I sign a 12-month corporate master lease at market rent, my tenants are 14+ night stays, fully insured, and I pay you the same whether the unit is occupied or not. The honest question: is this building zoned for transient lodging, or do you know if a Miami 21 BTR registration would be allowed here?"

### d) Newer Luxury Condo (Owner-Occupier Feel)

> "Hi [Name] — sounds like you take real pride in how this unit is kept, and you're probably skeptical of anyone calling about Airbnb. Fair. I'm not doing Airbnb — I master-lease furnished units for 12 months and place traveling executives and medical professionals on 30+ night stays. We carry $2M liability, do full background checks, and the unit stays in your name. Before I go further: does the HOA permit lease terms of 30 days or more?"

## 4. The Florida Lease Addendum — 5 Clauses

*Attach to landlord's master lease. Reference as "Addendum A — Furnished Housing Operations."*

**Clause 1 — Authorized Use & Sub-Occupancy Consent.**
Landlord expressly consents to Tenant's use of the Premises for furnished mid-term housing operations, including occupancy by sub-occupants under stays of thirty (30) consecutive nights or greater. Tenant remains the sole leaseholder and sole rent-paying party throughout the Term. This consent satisfies Florida Statute §83.20(2) restrictions on assignment and sublease.

**Clause 2 — Florida Statute §509.032 & Municipal Compliance.**
Tenant shall, at Tenant's sole expense, secure and maintain any registration, license, or permit required under Florida Statute §509.032, Miami-Dade County Ordinance Chapter 17, and applicable City of Miami / Miami Beach short-term and transient lodging codes (including Miami 21 §3.14 BTR registration where applicable). Tenant shall provide copies of all registrations to Landlord within thirty (30) days of issuance.

**Clause 3 — Insurance & Indemnification.**
Tenant shall maintain commercial general liability insurance of not less than $2,000,000 per occurrence naming Landlord as additional insured, plus a furnished-unit contents and host-liability policy covering all sub-occupants. Tenant indemnifies and holds Landlord harmless from any claim arising from sub-occupant conduct, except claims arising from Landlord's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

**Clause 4 — Municipal Registration & Tax Remittance.**
Tenant shall register the Premises with the appropriate municipal authority prior to first sub-occupancy and shall be solely responsible for collection and remittance of Florida Transient Rental Tax (6%), Miami-Dade Convention Development Tax (3%), and any applicable Tourist Development Tax. Tenant shall furnish quarterly proof of remittance upon Landlord's written request.

**Clause 5 — Adaptive Termination & Regulatory Change.**
If, during the Term, a change in state, county, or municipal law materially restricts Tenant's permitted use under Clause 1, either party may terminate this Addendum upon sixty (60) days' written notice without penalty. The underlying master lease shall convert to a standard residential tenancy at the then-current rent for the balance of the Term, and Tenant's deposit shall remain in place.
