[CORE BUSINESS MODEL]
Airbnb Arbitrage / STR Subleasing: lease properties from landlords, furnish and operate as short-term rentals, profit from the spread between rent and STR revenue.

Primary goals:
1. Acquire profitable STR properties with favorable lease terms
2. Minimize startup costs and time-to-live

4. Scale portfolio rapidly with operational leverage
5. Generate recurring monthly cash flow

[DOUBLE RENT RULE — UNDERWRITING ANCHOR]
Annual Projected Revenue must be at least 2x annual rent.
Formula: Monthly Rent x 12 x 2 = Minimum Target Revenue
Example: $2,500/mo x 12 = $30,000 → need $60,000+ in STR revenue to qualify.
Use AirDNA or BNBCalc to validate. Reject deals that don't clear this threshold.

[ACKERMAN RENT NEGOTIATION SEQUENCE]
Use this when negotiating monthly rent with a landlord.

1. Set your target rent using the 2x rule as the anchor (target = max you'll pay).
2. Open at 65% of target.
3. Counter 1: move to 85% of target.
4. Counter 2: move to 95% of target.
5. Final offer: 100% of target — use a non-round number (e.g., $2,187 not $2,200) and include one small non-monetary concession (longer lease term, faster move-in, or a minor maintenance offer).
6. Never exceed target. If they won't move, walk away gracefully: "I respect that — it just doesn't work at that number for me. If anything changes, I'd love to revisit it."

[OBJECTION FRAMEWORK — LAIRC]
Label → Acknowledge → Isolate → Resolve → Close

Step 1 — Label the emotion first (Voss):
"It sounds like you're concerned about..." or "It seems like this feels risky to you..."
Let them confirm or correct before you respond. This builds trust and slows the conversation down.

Step 2 — Acknowledge:
Validate the concern without agreeing it's a dealbreaker.
"That's a fair thing to think about."

Step 3 — Isolate:
"Besides that, is there anything else that would prevent you from moving forward?"
Never resolve an objection without isolating first — there may be a deeper one underneath.

Step 4 — Resolve:
Answer the real objection with proof, not just reassurance.
Use the Value Positioning points below.

Step 5 — Close:
Re-ask the question that got the objection.
"So with that addressed — does Thursday work to sign?"

[COMMON LANDLORD OBJECTIONS]
"I don't want strangers in my property."
Label: "It sounds like you've had bad experiences with tenants, or you're worried about wear and tear."
Resolve: Noise monitors, Ring cameras, smart locks, $1M liability insurance per unit, professional cleaning after every stay. More oversight than a standard tenant.

"I prefer a long-term tenant."
Label: "It sounds like stability and predictability are what matter most to you."
Resolve: Guaranteed rent paid 2 weeks in advance. Never a late payment. One point of contact, one lease — simpler than managing a traditional tenant.

"What if Airbnb bans your listings?"
Label: "It sounds like you're worried about being left holding a vacant unit."
Resolve: We list on Airbnb, VRBO, and direct booking. Diversified. And our lease terms protect you regardless of platform status.

[VALUE POSITIONING — BETTER THAN A NORMAL TENANT]
- Rent paid 2 weeks in advance, every month
- Professional cleaning after every guest stay
- Noise monitors + Ring cameras + smart locks installed at our cost
- $1M liability insurance per unit
- Property never sits vacant — we manage occupancy actively
- One leaseholder, one point of contact, no subletting drama
