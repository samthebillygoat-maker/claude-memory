## Tax Whistleblower Programs — Research Notes (2026-05-20 evening)

### IRS Whistleblower Program (Form 211)
- **Payout**: 15–30% of what IRS collects (mandatory if >$2M in unpaid taxes)
- **Timeline**: 7–10 years — the main drawback
- **Largest award ever**: $104M to Bradley Birkenfeld (UBS offshore accounts, 2012) — he also did 40 months in prison
- **Total paid out since 2007**: $1.1B+ to ~2,500 whistleblowers
- No anonymous submissions — IRS requires your identity

### Best State Program: New York False Claims Act
- Explicitly covers tax fraud (rare — most states don't)
- **Payout**: 15–25%, threshold $350K+ unpaid
- **Timeline**: 2–5 years (much faster than IRS)
- Sprint case: $330M settlement → **$62M to the whistleblower** in 4 years
- Most actionable angle if target has NY operations

### Best Fraud Types to Report (highest reward probability)
1. Offshore accounts / foreign asset non-disclosure (biggest awards historically)
2. Transfer pricing at large multinationals
3. Syndicated conservation easements (IRS hot priority right now, faster processing)
4. Payroll / worker misclassification fraud
5. Crypto non-disclosure (growing enforcement)

### Reality Check for STR Context
- Individual landlord income misreporting = too small to clear $2M IRS threshold
- Most actionable if Sam ever encounters a company with NY operations doing large-scale fraud
- Inheriting records of prior-owner tax fraud after acquiring a business = zero personal exposure + clean documentation (best low-risk scenario)

### Risks
- No anonymity at IRS level
- Retaliation protections exist but job loss is likely anyway
- Award is ordinary income — lose 45–55% to taxes at high award levels
- If you participated in the fraud, you have personal criminal exposure (Birkenfeld went to prison AND got $104M)
