# Rule

When pitching landlords for STR arbitrage, **lead with explicit disclosure**: Sam's operation includes Airbnb / VRBO / Furnished Finder listings, signed lease addendum is the owner's protection, and CitySide Stays LLC carries all STR permits, business licenses, lodging taxes, and full indemnity to the owner. **Do NOT claim "30+ night corporate housing only" or "we are not a short-term rental operator"** if actual stays are mixed (short + mid + corporate). That's material misrepresentation and creates fraud-in-inducement exposure on the master lease.

**Why:** Sam confirmed 2026-05-08 that his real operation is mixed STR + corporate, not pure MTR. The v1 emails and v1 VA script (both dated 2026-05-08 morning) leaned on "30+ nights typical / not a short-term rental" framing — they had to be pulled and rewritten before send. A landlord who signs a lease in reliance on "no STR / corporate only" and later discovers Airbnb listings has clean grounds to terminate, claim damages, and report fraudulent inducement.

**How to apply:** All future landlord outreach must use the honest mixed-use frame:

1. **Explicit channel disclosure** — name Airbnb, VRBO, Furnished Finder up front.
2. **Mixed guest mix** — "leisure and business travelers, traveling nurses and project managers on 30+ night contracts, and relocating professionals. Stays range from a few nights to several months."
3. **Indemnity is the trust builder** — "All STR permits, business licenses, and lodging taxes are our responsibility, with full indemnity to the owner." This is what makes risk-averse PMs relax.
4. **Adaptive-termination clause** — 30-day clean exit if local STR rules change. This kills the "stuck mid-lease when city bans STR" fear.
5. **Signed addendum** — every owner consents in writing before any listing goes live. The addendum's STR Compliance & Indemnity clause is non-negotiable.

Applies to: cold emails, VA call scripts (John), inbound objection handling, the Overview PDF, the Lease Addendum, Loom training assets, any future landlord-facing copy.
