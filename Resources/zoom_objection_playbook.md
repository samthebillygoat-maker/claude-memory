# Zoom Objection Playbook — Affiliate Pitch

**Pre-call frame:** You're not selling leads. You're selling his students a results multiplier on the methodology HE already teaches. 30% rev-share = recurring passive income on a $147/wk SKU.

---

## 1. "How is this different from PropStream?"

**Real concern:** Is this just another scraped-data tool I've already seen pitched 50 times?

**Response:** "PropStream gives you the LLC. We give you the human behind the LLC — the actual decision-maker, their direct cell, and why they're STR-friendly right now. Your students stop leaving voicemails for shell companies."

---

## 2. "What's your conversion rate to actual booked deals?"

**Real concern:** If I put my name on this and it doesn't convert, my students churn.

**Response:** "Depends entirely on the operator's call game — which is what YOU teach. What I can tell you is response rate: roughly 1 in 8 leads books a call when the operator uses the corporate-housing framing. The deal close is on the rep."

---

## 3. "How do I know these leads aren't recycled / sold to competitors?"

**Real concern:** I don't want my students racing 200 other people to the same landlord.

**Response:** "Every Friday Pack is a fresh 50, sourced that week, and we cap distribution per market. I'll put a contractual market-cap clause in the affiliate agreement if that unlocks this — name your number."

---

## 4. "My students already have a lead system that works. Why switch?"

**Real concern:** I don't want to disrupt what's working or admit there's a gap.

**Response:** "Not asking them to switch — asking them to stack. The students who already cold-call great will close 2-3x more from a Friday Pack because the contact data is already enriched; it's a force multiplier on their existing system, not a replacement."

---

## 5. "30% is low — I want 50%." ⭐ NEGOTIATION

**Real concern:** Anchoring to test if you'll fold. Probably happy at 38-42%.

**Ackerman sequence (anchor low, walk up in shrinking increments, end on an odd number + non-monetary throw-in):**

> **Anchor:** **30%** (already on the table)
> **Counter 1:** "I can't do 50, but I can stretch to **35%** if we lock a 12-month exclusive in your coaching program."
> **Counter 2:** "Let's split the difference toward your number — **38%**, same 12-month lock."
> **Final (Ackerman odd-number close + non-cash sweetener):** "**41.5%** is my ceiling, and I'll throw in white-label Friday Packs co-branded with your program for the first 90 days. That's my last number."

**If he still pushes:** "It sounds like the percentage is the dealbreaker here. How am I supposed to go higher than 41.5 when I'm sourcing, enriching, and supporting every lead?" Then silence.

---

## 6. "What if my students complain about the quality?"

**Real concern:** Reputation risk. He's protecting his brand, not the students.

**Response:** "Every pack has a 7-day no-questions refund — students refund to me, not to you, so your brand never eats it. I'd rather lose $147 than have you fielding angry DMs about my product."

---

## 7. "I need to see your traffic / customer numbers first." ⭐ CONCEDE

**Real concern:** Is this a real business or a side project?

**CONCEDE SMALL, PIVOT TO METHODOLOGY:** "Fair — we're early, under 50 paying subs and growing weekly, I won't dress it up. The real asset isn't the customer count, it's the owner-grouping methodology — let me share my screen and show you how we surface the human behind a 12-LLC portfolio in under 90 seconds. That's what nobody else has."

---

## 8. "Can I have an exclusive in [his region]?"

**Real concern:** Testing leverage AND signaling he's seriously interested (buying signal).

**Response:** "Regional exclusive is on the table, but it has to come with a volume floor — say, 25 paid student sign-ups in the first 60 days. If you hit it, the region is yours and the rev-share bumps; if not, it opens back up."

---

## 9. "How long have you been doing this?"

**Real concern:** Are you a tourist or a real operator?

**Response:** "I run CitySide Stays as a national arbitrage operation — Friday Pack came out of the exact lead problem my own acquisitions team had. I built the tool I needed; the subscription product is six months old, the methodology is two years."

---

## 10. "What happens if you stop running this in 6 months?"

**Real concern:** Continuity risk on his recurring rev-share.

**Response:** "It sounds like you're worried about leaving your students stranded. If Friday Pack ever sunsets, every affiliate gets 60 days notice plus the full lead database as a parting gift — that's in the contract."

---

# Tells

## ✅ Going well — coach is sold, push for close:
1. **"Walk me through how onboarding would work for my students..."** (he's mentally selling it already)
2. **"What's the soonest we could pilot this with my next cohort?"** (timeline questions = buying)
3. **"Send me the affiliate agreement to look over"** (asking for paper = green light, get it out same day)

## 🚨 Going badly — pivot to free-pack ask before he ghosts:
1. **"Let me think about it and circle back"** (no specific date = no)
2. **"This is interesting but my plate is really full right now"** (polite kill)
3. **He stops asking questions and just nods** (mentally checked out — re-engage with a question, not another pitch)

**Pivot script when you see a kill signal:**
> "Totally hear you. Look, no pressure on the affiliate piece — can I just send you a free Friday Pack for your own market this week? You eyeball the quality, if it's a fit we talk again, if not no hard feelings."

That gets him holding the product for free. 80% of "no" coaches who take the free pack come back within 14 days.
