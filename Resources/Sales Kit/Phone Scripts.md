# Phone Scripts

# Phone & Voicemail Scripts

When a contractor texts/emails back saying "call me" or your number rings unexpectedly, don't wing it. Use these.

---

## Inbound: contractor calls you

**They say:** "Hey, I got your text — I'm {name} from {biz}."

**You say:**
> Hey {name}, thanks for calling back. So I sent you a quick site mockup for {biz} — did it come through OK?

(They'll say yes or no.)

**If yes:**
> Cool. So I'm a 19-year-old college student teaching myself web design. I've been making sample sites for licensed CA contractors. The mockup I sent you took me about 20 minutes — what you'd actually get for $300 is exactly that, deployed live on your domain in 5 days. Does that feel useful to you?

**If no / didn't see it:**
> No worries — give me your email and I'll resend. While you're on the line — what's frustrating about your current site?

(Listen. Take notes. Their pain = your pitch.)

---

## Inbound: contractor calls and you can't pick up

Voicemail greeting:
> Hey, you've reached Sam Lieberman. I make websites for CA trade contractors. Leave your business name and I'll call you back today. Thanks.

(15 seconds. Friendly. Not robotic.)

---

## Outbound: you call them (after they reply YES via email)

**Open with:**
> Hi {name}, this is Sam Lieberman — I sent you the site mockup for {biz} and you said you wanted to talk. Did I catch you at an OK time?

**If yes:**
> Cool. Three things to confirm fast: (1) you saw the mockup and like the direction, (2) you want it live on your own domain, (3) for Tier 1 it's $150 deposit + $150 on launch (50/50 split of the $300 total). Sound right?

**If they hesitate at any of those, just listen.** Don't sell over their concern. Address what they actually said.

**If they're ready:**
> Great. I'll send you the agreement and Stripe payment link in the next 10 minutes. Sign + pay the deposit, and I start tonight. Site goes live by {target_date}. Sound good?

---

## Outbound: voicemail when they don't answer

> Hi {name}, this is Sam Lieberman — sent you the website mockup for {biz}. Just trying to connect for 5 minutes about whether it's useful. My number is {your_number}. Or just text me when you have a sec. Thanks.

(20 seconds. Don't pitch on voicemail. Just confirm who you are and offer a path back.)

---

## When they ask "have you done this before?"

> Honestly, I'm new — that's why the rate is what it is. I'm building a portfolio. The mockup I sent you isn't my first one — I've made about 50 (or however many you've actually made). What I bring is fresh design taste and the willingness to iterate until you actually like it. If that's not the right fit, totally fair to say no.

(Don't lie. Don't hide. Lean into being new + cheap. That's the trade.)

---

## When they ask "why are you doing this?"

> Two things — I'm in college and need money, and I'm teaching myself web design. Building real sites for real businesses is the only way to learn. Working with contractors specifically because (a) most agencies overcharge you, and (b) your sites actually need to load on mobile when someone's roof is leaking — and most of the templates out there don't.

---

## When they get aggressive ("how did you get my number")

Stay calm:
> Totally fair to ask. CSLB publishes contractor business phones publicly at cslb.ca.gov — I pulled the list of recently licensed roofers in California. I message each one once with a sample. If you don't want to hear from me again, just say so and I'll never contact you. Want me to remove you?

If yes, do it. Add to suppression list. Move on. Never re-engage.

---

## Pre-call checklist (when you've scheduled a call)

- Open the contractor's mockup in a tab so you can refer to it
- Open `out/competitive_intel/{license}.md` so you know their site weaknesses
- Have Stripe Payment Links open and ready to paste
- Have `sales/SERVICE_AGREEMENT.md` ready to copy-fill
- Quiet room, headphones on, water within reach
- Smile while talking — they hear it
