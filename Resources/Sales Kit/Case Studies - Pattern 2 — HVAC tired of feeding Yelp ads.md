# Pattern 2 — HVAC tired of feeding Yelp ads

## Context
From [[Case Studies]].

## Content

**Common situation**: Family-owned HVAC, 8+ years, 2-4 trucks, paying Yelp $300-2,000/mo for ads. Yelp page is the public-facing presence. Own website is an embarrassment they hide.

**What Tier 3 build typically delivers**:
- Estimate-request form with photo upload (customer texts a pic of their broken unit)
- "24/7 Emergency" red banner with click-to-call (huge for HVAC)
- Local SEO content: 5-10 blog posts targeting "AC repair in [their cities]"
- Google Business Profile claimed and linked
- Direct booking via embedded calendar (Calendly-style)

**What changes**: a real website lets them shift Yelp ad spend to organic Google leads over 2-3 months. Their own brand reasserts itself instead of every job being attributed to "Yelp." Booked-online jobs reduce phone-tag time.

**Total cost**: $1,500 + optional $40/mo retainer for content.
