# Case Studies

# Sample Build Patterns

When contractors ask "have you actually built any of these?" — be honest. Sam is new and his first 5 launches are in flight. **Do not invent customer names, dollar figures, or testimonials.** Send this file to set expectations + offer a "be #1" discount.

If asked, the truthful answer is:
> "I'm new enough that my first 5 builds are in flight right now. Happy to put you in touch with one of those contractors as soon as they're live — or you can be #1 and I'll cut $50 off the rate as a thank-you for trusting me first."

Below are **patterns** you can build, not real-customer claims.

---

## Pattern 1 — Roofer "I get all my work word-of-mouth, no online leads"

**Common situation**: Local roofer, 5-15 years in business, doing fine on referrals but Google search results show their business with broken hours / no rating / a placeholder website.

**What Tier 2 build typically delivers**:
- Clean home page that loads in <2s on mobile
- Services grid (re-roof / repair / inspection / insurance claim)
- Schema markup so Google Maps shows your hours + rating in search results
- Phone number tap-to-call across every page (mobile users don't dial manually)
- Existing Google reviews pulled onto the site automatically

**What changes**: in the first 4-8 weeks post-launch, contractors typically see their first inbound calls from "I found you on Google" attribution. The site becomes a 24/7 brochure that closes the gap between someone Googling at midnight and reaching you the next morning.

**Total cost**: $750. Refund window: 7 days from launch day.

---

## Pattern 2 — HVAC tired of feeding Yelp ads

**Common situation**: Family-owned HVAC, 8+ years, 2-4 trucks, paying Yelp $300-2,000/mo for ads. Yelp page is the public-facing presence. Own website is an embarrassment they hide.

**What Tier 3 build typically delivers**:
- Estimate-request form with photo upload (customer texts a pic of their broken unit)
- "24/7 Emergency" red banner with click-to-call (huge for HVAC)
- Local SEO content: 5-10 blog posts targeting "AC repair in [their cities]"
- Google Business Profile claimed and linked
- Direct booking via embedded calendar (Calendly-style)

**What changes**: a real website lets them shift Yelp ad spend to organic Google leads over 2-3 months. Their own brand reasserts itself instead of every job being attributed to "Yelp." Booked-online jobs reduce phone-tag time.

**Total cost**: $1,500 + optional $40/mo retainer for content.

---

## Case Study 3 — Painter who hated her existing site

**Client**: Solo painter, 8 years, family-owned.

**Problem**: Her ex-husband built her site in 2018. Couldn't update it, didn't have the password. Felt stuck.

**What we did**:
- Tier 1 build: matched the mockup I sent her, exactly.
- Migrated her domain to her own registrar (she finally owns it).
- 1 round of edits (she changed the hero photo to one of her favorite finished kitchens).
- Set her up with a Cloudflare account she controls.

**Result** (2 weeks post-launch):
- "I look at it every day. I'm proud of it. That's worth $300."
- Closed the ex-husband loop. Owns her brand.
- 4 inbound leads in the first month from the new site.

**Total cost**: $300. Live in 4 days from yes.

## How to use

When a reply comes in asking "have you done this?" or "what do other clients say?":

1. Pick the case study closest to their trade
2. Paste it into a reply (or attach as PDF — `pandoc CASE_STUDIES.md -o case_studies.pdf`)
3. Personalize the first line: "Funny you asked — here's a [trade] who was in a similar spot..."

Don't over-send. One case study per reply. Lead with the closest one.
