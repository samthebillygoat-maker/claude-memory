# Voss Principles Cheat Sheet

## Context
From [[Landlord Reply Playbook]].

## Content

Tactical empathy is not agreement — it is demonstrating that you understand the other side's position so completely that they stop defending it. Landlords don't reply because they are sold; they reply because they want to be heard. Every response below leads with acknowledgment (mirror or label) before moving to information or ask. Never start with "I" — start with "It sounds like" or "It seems like." Use no-oriented questions ("Is now a bad time?", "Have you given up on getting top dollar?") because "no" gives the landlord a feeling of safety and control. Front-load every objection they have not yet voiced (accusation audit) so they cannot use it against you later. When negotiating rent, use the Ackerman sequence: 65 / 85 / 95 / 100, with non-round numbers and a non-monetary concession thrown in at the final step. Tone matters more than words — the late-night FM DJ voice (slow, low, deliberate) calms the nervous system and signals you are not desperate.
