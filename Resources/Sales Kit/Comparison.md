## The math at year 5

If you go Wix Premium today, in 5 years you've paid **$960** for a templated site you don't own and can't move.
You could pay me **$300 once** for a custom site you fully own, and put the other **$660** toward Google Local Service Ads, your truck, or a vacation.

## "But Wix is free"

Free Wix shows "This site was built with Wix" in the footer. Customers see it. Looks unserious.
Free Wix also doesn't let you use your own domain (it's `yourname.wixsite.com/business`). Looks even more unserious.

You can't have both "free" and "looks legit." Either pay Wix forever or pay me once.

## "I already started one on Squarespace, I just need to finish it"

Cool — but at $23/mo over 5 years, that's $1,380. My Tier 2 (5-page custom site, mobile-optimized, real SEO) is $750. You'd save $630 over 5 years AND get a better-built site.

If you want, I'll match the design direction you've started on Squarespace and just rebuild it as code you own. Send me the link.

## "I already use ServiceTitan / JobNimbus / Housecall Pro for my website"

Those are operations platforms with a website module bolted on. They're great for scheduling and dispatch. Their websites... aren't.

ServiceTitan websites all share a template — your site looks identical to your competitor's site. That's the opposite of differentiation.

I build a custom front door. You keep ServiceTitan / JobNimbus for the back office. Best of both.

## When to NOT hire me, in good faith

- You're under 1 year licensed and aren't sure you'll keep operating
- Your business name is going to change in the next 12 months
- You already have a site that ranks well + converts well — just upgrade it incrementally with a freelance dev for a few hours
- You're 100% referral-based and have never wanted online leads — Wix free is fine

## What I'm NOT trying to compete with

- Hook Agency / RoofingSites.com / similar specialists — they charge $5K-15K and deliver more. If your business clears $500K+/yr and you want lead-gen as a system, hire them.
- Your nephew who'll do it for free — if they're decent, take that deal. I'd rather see you launch something than worry about who built it.

## The honest pitch

I'm 19, in college, learning by doing. The mockup I sent you was 15 minutes of my time. The live build will be ~8 hours of my time over 5 days. At $300, that's not a charity rate, it's just a fair rate for someone who's still building a portfolio.

If 5 years from now you decide to upgrade, you'll have a real codebase you own — not a Wix account you've been renting.
