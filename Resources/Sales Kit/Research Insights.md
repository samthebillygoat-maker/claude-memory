# Research Insights

# Research Insights — What Actually Converts

Research conducted 2026-05-06. Sources cited inline. Read this once; apply forever.

## The single highest-leverage change

**Replace text-only emails with a 60-90 second personalized Loom recorded over their current website**, with a screenshot thumbnail embedded inline.

Why it stacks every multiplier:
- Visual personalization: 5x reply rate (3.4% → 18%)
- Video format: 4x reply rate in field tests (4% → 22%)
- Concrete proof-of-work over abstract claims

For a roofer who hates reading, watching you click through their broken mobile menu for 45 seconds is the single most persuasive artifact you can send.

**Subject line for this pattern:** "quick thing on {company} site" (lowercase, 4 words, references them specifically)

## Send-time data (CA trade contractors)

Trade owners are in trucks 7am-3pm PT. They check phones at lunch and after dinner. Override the generic "Tue 10am" rule:

**Email:**
- Best: Tue / Wed / Thu **6:30-7:30am PT** (before they roll)
- Or: **7-9pm PT** (evenings — 6.52% reply rate per Belkins)
- Avoid: Fri afternoon, weekends, Mon mornings (overflow inbox)

**SMS / MMS:**
- Best: Tue / Wed / Thu **11:30am-1pm PT** (lunch break)
- Or: **5-7pm PT** (after work)
- TCPA hard cap: 8am-9pm recipient local time, only

## Email format that wins

- Subject: 2-4 words, lowercase, question or first-name format
- Length: 75-100 words (51% response rate sweet spot per Artisan)
- Sentences: 6-8 max
- ONE call to action
- Problem-first positioning ("most contractor sites I see are missing X, costs you jobs")
- Reference a specific page/photo on their actual site in line 1

Length data: Lemlist found 120-word emails booked at 52% vs 20% for 300 words.

## SMS / MMS format that wins

- **Use MMS with the mockup image** — 52% higher CTR than SMS-only (SimpleTexting)
- Cost is ~3x SMS but irrelevant at our volume
- The mockup is the entire pitch — show, don't link
- Pair with a single specific question ("worth a quick look?")

## TCPA compliance — non-negotiable

Cold SMS to a cell number without consent = $500-$1,500 per message in damages. CSLB business-line publication is NOT consent (Van Patten v. Vertical Fitness).

**Every SMS / MMS must include:**
- Business name (sender ID): "Sam, Sam's Web Design"
- Purpose statement (what is this about)
- Opt-out: `Reply STOP to opt out`
- Help: `HELP for info` (in initial message at minimum)
- Implied: "Msg & data rates may apply" (helpful, not always required)

**Quiet hours:** 8am-9pm recipient local. Stick to **9am-6pm weekdays** to be safe + actually convert.

**Honor STOP within 10 business days** (FCC April 2025 rule). Honor ANY reasonable opt-out phrasing ("leave me alone," "unsubscribe," "remove me"), not just "STOP." Twilio handles this automatically — don't disable.

**Records to keep 4 years (TCPA SOL):**
- Timestamp sent
- Recipient number
- Exact message body
- Consent basis (we're using "publicly listed CSLB business contact for B2B website outreach")
- Opt-out timestamp (if they reply STOP)
- Internal DNC list

## Subject line patterns ranked by open rate

From Belkins / Instantly benchmark data:
1. **"Hi {first_name}"** — 45.4% open
2. **Question format** — 46% open ("worth a quick look?", "useful or skip?")
3. **Trigger event reference** — +45% lift ("noticed your site doesn't load on mobile")
4. **Lowercase, 2-4 words** — beats title case, beats long subjects
5. **AVOID**: "ASAP," "URGENT," "Free," "Limited time," "ACT NOW"

Currently we use: `A landing page mockup for {biz}` — that's 6 words, title-case, generic. **Switch to:** `quick thing on {biz} site` or `{first_name} - 2-min thing` or `mockup for {biz}` (just 4 words).

## Personalization layers (ranked)

The biggest reply-rate jumps come from showing you actually looked at the recipient. In order of leverage:
1. **Reference something specific from their actual site/photos** (5x lift)
2. **Use their first name in the body** (1.5x)
3. **Mention their city by name** (we already do this)
4. **Reference their license year** (we already do this)
5. **Generic AI-personalized greeting** ("Hi there" / "Hey friend") — actively hurts; sounds like spam

## Volume strategy

- Big lists (1000+) reply at 2.1%
- Small lists (≤50 hyper-targeted) reply at 5.8%
- We're doing 30-50/day = right in the sweet spot. Don't scale past 100/day until reply rate confirms quality.

## Practical checklist for tomorrow's outreach

- [ ] Subject line 2-4 words, lowercase, references them
- [ ] 75-100 word body, 6-8 sentences, single CTA
- [ ] Inline mockup image (no Loom for v1, save for v2)
- [ ] Send Tue/Wed/Thu 6:30am OR 7pm PT
- [ ] First line references something specific to them
- [ ] No subject line spam-words ("Free", "ASAP", "Limited")
- [ ] Suppression list checked before each send
- [ ] Daily cap of 30-40
- [ ] Track reply rate per subject variant
