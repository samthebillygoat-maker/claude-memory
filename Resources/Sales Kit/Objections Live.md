# Objections Live

# Live Objections — phone/text quick reference

Use during a call when you have 4 seconds of dead air. Scan, find their objection, read the response. Different from FAQ.md (that's for emails — paragraphs). This is bullets you can read while the line is silent.

---

| What they say | What they actually mean | Your 1-line response + redirect |
|---|---|---|
| "Let me think about it" | Not enough info to decide / scared | "Totally — what's the one thing that would make this a yes for you?" |
| "How much again?" | Stalling / hopes for a discount | "$300 for Tier 1, $750 for Tier 2, $1,500 for Tier 3 — which one fits your situation?" |
| "Send me more info" | Not ready / wants to ghost | "Sent it already — you got the mockup. What specifically would help you decide?" |
| "I'll talk to my wife/partner/business partner" | Real check OR polite no | "Cool — when's a good time we can do a 5-min call together so I can answer their questions live?" |
| "My nephew/cousin can do it for free" | Price-anchored / loyalty | "Great — and if they ship in the next 6 months, perfect. If not, my $300 is for the speed and the money-back guarantee." |
| "Can you do it cheaper?" | Wants a deal | "$300 is already below market — but if you want to be one of my first 5 launches, I'll knock $50 off in exchange for a testimonial after we ship." |
| "I already have a website" | Defensive / proud / actually does | "I saw it — what specifically about it works for you? If anything's missing, that's where I'd help." |
| "I'm too busy right now" | Overwhelmed / not the priority | "Totally fair. What I built takes 5 days of MY time, not yours — you spend 30 minutes total. When does a 30-min slot open up for you?" |
| "I don't trust online stuff" | Skeptical / scammed before | "I get it. The $300 is fully refundable for 7 days from launch. If it doesn't work for you, you get the money back, no questions." |
| "What if I don't like it?" | Risk aversion | "Then we iterate — Tier 1 includes one revision round. After that I refund the full $300." |
| "Can my buddy maintain it?" | Future flexibility worry | "Yeah — code is plain HTML/CSS/JS, anyone with web skills can edit it. I write it readable, not minified." |
| "Why should I trust a 19-year-old?" | Age skepticism | "Honestly the trade is — I'm cheaper because I'm new. Ship the live site in 5 days with a 7-day money-back guarantee. Lower risk than waiting." |
| "How many of these have you done?" | Validation | "You'd be #2 [or whatever the real number is]. That's why the rate is what it is — by next year it's $600+." |
| "Can I see one you've actually built?" | Wants social proof | "My first 5 launches are in flight — happy to put you in touch with one as soon as they're live, or you can be #1 and I'll cut $50 off." |
| "What if you go back to school full-time?" | Continuity worry | "Code lives on YOUR Cloudflare account in your name. If I disappear tomorrow, your site keeps running. Any web dev on Upwork can pick it up." |
| "I don't want to give you my domain login" | Trust | "You shouldn't have to. Cloudflare and your registrar both let you add me as a collaborator without sharing logins. I'll send you the steps." |
| "Will you do logos / business cards / Yelp / Facebook?" | Wants the whole thing | "No — websites only. Logos I'd recommend a specialist; cards I can recommend a printer; Yelp/FB you handle. I do one thing well." |
| "What's the cheapest you'll go?" | Bargain-hunter | "$300 is already the floor. If $300 is too much, this isn't the right fit — fair?" |
| "Send the contract" | Ready to sign | "Sending now — opens in 30 seconds." (Then actually send it within 60 seconds. Speed = trust.) |
| "Why are you so cheap?" | Suspicious | "I'm 19 in college, building a portfolio. By next summer the rate is $600+. This is the cheapest you'll ever see me." |

## Universal redirect when stuck

> "Cool — what's the one specific thing that would make this a 'yes' for you right now?"

If they can answer it, you know what to fix. If they can't, they're not buying — politely close out and don't waste either of your time.

## Universal close

> "Okay let's do it. I'll text you the agreement and a Stripe link in the next 10 minutes. Sign + pay the deposit, and I start tonight. Site live by [date]. Sound good?"

## When to walk away

If they've said "I need to think about it" twice, or if they're trying to negotiate below $300, or if they're asking you to do work outside scope (logo, business cards, etc), respond once kindly and let it go. Bad-fit clients eat 80% of your time for 20% of the revenue.

> "Totally fair — sounds like the timing isn't right. If anything changes, my number's the same. Take care."
