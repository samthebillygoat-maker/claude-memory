# Faq

# FAQ — Pre-emptive answers

For when a contractor asks the obvious question. Paste verbatim or adapt.

---

### "Is this real or a scam?"

It's real. I'm Sam Lieberman, a 19-year-old college student in California (sole proprietor, address in my email footer). I'm not an agency, I don't have employees, and I'm not selling anything ongoing — one flat fee, you own the site forever. The mockup you got was custom-rendered for your business specifically, not a template.

If it would help, I can hop on a 5-minute call so you can put a voice/face to the name.

---

### "How did you get my information?"

The California Contractors State License Board (cslb.ca.gov) publishes a public list of all licensed contractors with their business phone and address. I pulled that list and filtered to recently-licensed roofers. I message each contractor once with a custom mockup. If you want me to remove your contact info, just reply STOP or "remove me" and I'll do it.

---

### "Why $300 for a website? That seems low."

Two reasons:
1. **I'm new** — I'm 19 and teaching myself by building real sites for real businesses. Cheaper rate is the trade for working with someone with less experience.
2. **No overhead** — I'm one person. No agency, no project manager, no markup. Just my time.

Honestly, when I get good enough, the rate goes up. So this is the cheapest you'll ever see me.

---

### "What if I don't like it?"

Tier 1 ($300) has a 7-day money-back guarantee from launch. If you genuinely don't like it, I refund the full $300 (I eat the Stripe processing fee). That's how confident I am.

Tier 2 and 3 are refundable up to launch day at 50% (the deposit). After launch, no refund — but I'll keep iterating until it's right within your included revision rounds.

---

### "Do I have to pay a monthly fee?"

No. The base tier is one-time, you own the code, hosted free on Cloudflare Pages.

The only optional ongoing thing is a $40/mo retainer if you want me to handle ongoing edits, content updates, and monitoring. Skip it if you want — your nephew can edit the HTML, or you can just leave it as is.

---

### "Will I be locked in?"

No. After launch:
- Domain is registered to you (I'll transfer it if I bought it for you)
- Code is on your Cloudflare account, which I set up under your email
- Anyone with basic web skills can edit it — I write readable HTML, not minified spaghetti
- I'll give you a 1-page handoff doc explaining how to make basic changes

If you want to fire me and migrate to someone else, you can do it in an afternoon.

---

### "What if I already have a domain?"

Easy. Just give me the registrar (GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc.) and I'll point it at the new site. You don't lose your existing email or anything — only the website at that domain changes.

---

### "Can you also do my logo / business cards / Yelp / Facebook / SEO?"

- **Logo**: no — I'll use what you have, or a clean text logo. Logo design is its own skill, and you're better off paying a logo specialist directly ($100-300).
- **Business cards**: no, but I can recommend a printer.
- **Yelp / Facebook**: no — those are managed in their own dashboards. I can advise.
- **Basic SEO** (title tags, meta descriptions, fast load): **yes**, all tiers.
- **Local SEO content** (blog posts targeting "[trade] in [city]"): **Tier 3 only**.
- **Paid ads / Google Ads**: no. Honestly Google Local Service Ads beat custom-managed ad campaigns for trades.

---

### "How long does it take?"

- Tier 1 ($300): 5 business days from go-ahead
- Tier 2 ($750): 10 business days
- Tier 3 ($1,500): 15 business days

If I miss the timeline by more than 5 business days because of stuff I can control, you get a 10% refund per week of delay.

---

### "What if I want changes after launch?"

Each tier includes revision rounds (1 / 2 / 3 by tier). After those, $75 per additional revision (one revision = up to 5 specific changes).

If you take the $40/mo retainer, edits are included.

---

### "Can my nephew/cousin maintain it?"

Yes — code is plain HTML/CSS/JS, anyone with basic web skills can edit. No proprietary CMS, no theme builder, no plugin hellscape. I write it readable.

---

### "Can you put my Yelp/Google reviews on the site?"

**Yes.** Tier 2 and 3 pull live reviews from your Google Business Profile or Yelp page directly into a section on the site. Tier 1 includes static testimonials you write or that I pull manually.

---

### "Do you do photos?"

Photography is on you. If you don't have photos, I'll use stock photos relevant to your trade (free for commercial use). My favorite move is to scrape your own Yelp/Facebook page for photos you've already shared and use those — feels personal without you having to dig through your phone.

---

### "Are you insured?"

I carry $1M general liability through {your_insurer}. Web design is low-physical-risk so this is mostly performance-bond level coverage. Happy to send a certificate.

(Note for Sam: if you don't actually have business insurance, leave this question off. Don't lie. As you scale, $30/mo via Hiscox or Next Insurance gets you a $1M general liability policy.)

---

### "What if Cloudflare Pages goes away?"

The site is plain HTML/CSS/JS. If Cloudflare ever shut down (extremely unlikely), I can redeploy the same files on Vercel, Netlify, GitHub Pages, or any web host with a 5-minute config change. You're not locked into Cloudflare specifically.

---

### "What about a payment plan?"

50% deposit / 50% on launch is already a payment plan. If you need more flexibility (3 payments, etc.), text me and we'll figure it out — I'd rather work with you than lose the deal.
