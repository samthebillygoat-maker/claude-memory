## Airbnb ToS April 20 2026

All pre-Feb-5-2026 accounts had to accept on April 20 or lose access to bookings and payouts.

**Key changes:**
- Airbnb can **freeze host payouts indefinitely** — no explanation, no timeline, based on undefined "risk factors"
- AI-generated/enhanced photos **banned from AirCover damage claims**
- Consumables (toiletries, cleaning supplies) **ineligible for AirCover**
- Host cancellation penalties now **10–50% of reservation** (was flat $50/$100), $50 minimum
- Dispute resolution switched to **AAA** (~$225 filing fee, recoverable on win)
- 24-hour free cancellation now applies to **all listings** regardless of host cancellation policy
- **Class action waiver** extended to Canadian users

**Airbnb Summer Release 2026 (mid-2026):** AI-powered host tools, natural language search, third-party marketplace integration. Differentiate on hyperlocal expertise before Airbnb commoditizes.

## Regulations — 2026 Updates

**Miami-Dade:**
- Tourist tax remittance now **MONTHLY** (was quarterly) — 25% penalty for late
- TouristExpress online filing **mandatory** since Oct 2025 (paper = extra fees)
- Business Tax Receipt (BTR) required if >**$25,000/yr** rental income
- Occupancy cap: **max 2 per bedroom + 2 additional, absolute max 12 persons** (children under 3 excluded)
- Active STR listings: ~28,000 (+12% YoY) — market increasingly competitive

**City of Miami / State:**
- Licensing costs: ~$500–800 initial · ~$300/yr renewal
  - DBPR Vacation Rental License: ~$170/yr + $50 one-time
  - FL Dept of Revenue sales tax: free
  - Miami-Dade Certificate of Use: varies
  - City Business Tax Receipt: varies

**Miami Beach:**
- Minimum **500-foot distance** between licensed STR properties in single-family residential zones
- **Grandfathering cutoff Jan 1, 2026** — new applications now much harder
- Building **automated compliance platform** to auto-detect unlicensed listings via Airbnb/VRBO APIs
- Fine starting at $20k, repeat offenders up to **$100k**

**STR-friendly condos (rare):**
- LOFTY Brickell (99 SW 7th St) — purpose-built, T6 zoning, no nightly rate cap
- The Standard Brickell (1034 SW 2nd Ave) — fully STR-certified
