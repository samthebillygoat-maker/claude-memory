## Section 122

**Rate: 15%** (NOT 10% — Trump raised it from 10% to 15% on Feb 22 via social media, just before Feb 24 effective date)

- **Expires:** July 24, 2026 unless Congress votes to extend (150-day window from Feb 24)
- **Court challenge:** CIT 3-judge panel (Barnett/Kelly/Stanceu) held "marathon hearing" Apr 10, 2026 — judges "sharply probed" legality, adversarial posture toward government · no ruling yet
- 24 states + Liberty Justice Center filed for preliminary injunction AND summary judgment — if granted, collection halts immediately
- Lead case switched to Euro-Notions Florida, Inc. v. CBP, No. 25-00595 (Apr 7) · government appeal deadline ~Jun 6, 2026

**Exemptions:**
- USMCA-qualifying goods from Canada and Mexico
- Section 232-covered goods (no double-stacking 122+232)
- Critical minerals, natural resources not available in US, select ag products, medical/pharma, certain electronics, vehicles/transport/parts, aerospace

**Ecuador:** Fully exempt from Section 122 and reciprocal tariffs (US-Ecuador agreement Mar 13, 2026)

## Section 232 — Metals (Effective April 6, 2026)

Restructured from prior rules — now applies to **full customs value**, not just declared metal content.

| Category | Rate |
|---|---|
| Primary aluminum, steel, most copper (Annex I-A) | 50% |
| Derivative articles substantially made of these metals (Annex I-B) | 25% |
| Metal-intensive industrial/electrical grid equipment | 15% transitional through Dec 31, 2027 |
| Products made entirely from US-origin metal | 10% |
| Products ≤15% metal content by value | Exempt |

Must recalculate Section 232 exposure for all derivative metal goods under new tiered structure.

## CAPE System — IEEPA Tariff Refunds

**Launched April 20, 2026** (with technical glitches — "high volume" errors, "Duplicate tax ID" errors · CBP support calls April 21 at 1pm ET)

**How it works:**
- Upload CSV of entry summary numbers via "CAPE Claim Portal" tab in ACE Portal
- Requires ACH refund enrollment (SEPARATE from duty-payment ACH — must enroll under "ACH Refund Authorization" tab)
- Each Declaration capped at 9,999 entries · file multiple Declarations for larger sets
- **Cannot be amended once accepted** — missed entries need a new Declaration
- Processing time: **60–90 days** after acceptance (not 45 as initially reported)

**Who can file Phase 1:**
- Only original Importer of Record (IOR) or the customs broker who filed the entries
- Third-party consultants who did NOT file entries cannot submit — but IOR can designate them to RECEIVE the refund via CBP Form 4811

**Phase 1 exclusions:**
- AD/CVD-subject products
- Entries flagged for reconciliation (common with Chinese-affiliate transfer pricing)
- Entries where drawback claim already filed
- Entries with open/pending protests

**Phase 2** (finalized/liquidated entries — ~37% of IEEPA duty entries): **No announced timeline**
