## The free 50 — how to offer it

**DON'T** offer "I'll send you 50 leads" up front. Position it as a callback action:

> "Pick a market — Tampa, Smokies, Gulf Shores, Chattanooga, or Branson — and I'll send you
> the next Friday Pack for that market on the house. Same delivery your students would get.
> If the quality clicks, let's talk affiliate next week."

Reasons:
1. Makes them choose / engage / commit.
2. Gives you a reason to follow up Monday.
3. Sets the affiliate conversation as the natural next step.

If they pick Tampa, you can ship them today's live run output (currently churning in the
background, ~110 phones expected) PLUS the previous Tampa-10 sample as a "v1 vs v2" credibility move.

---

## What NOT to say

- ❌ Don't promise volumes or conversion rates you can't back up.
- ❌ Don't mention any past Friday Pack screw-ups (Stripe issues, launch night). Old news.
- ❌ Don't say "we" if you're solo — they'll see through it. Say "I" — "small operator" beats
  "fake team."
- ❌ Don't say "we have AI" — say "I use an LLM for one specific step: extracting owner names
  from listing bodies. The rest is structured data work."
- ❌ Don't drift into the SaaS-portfolio pitch (Hail Mary, DealMemo, etc.). One product per call.
- ❌ Don't reference Kash by name unless they bring him up.

## Closing the call (last 5 min)

Three things to nail:
1. **Get a clear next step.** "When do we talk affiliate?" → calendar invite for next week.
2. **Send the free pack same day.** Don't wait. Momentum dies in 48 hours.
3. **Ask if you can quote his feedback.** Even informal: "Friday Pack's leads are the best
   I've seen for STR arbitrage" is gold for the site.

## If the call goes well — drop this last:

> "One more thing. If you ever want to white-label Friday Pack for your coaching program —
> your branding, your delivery email, your pricing — I'm open to talking. Lower margin per
> lead, but volume is recurring."

This is the "yes-now" play. Affiliates think transactional; white-label thinks long-term.

---

## If the call goes badly

Don't argue. Listen for the objection. Say:
> "That's fair. Let me think on it and follow up Monday. Either way — what market should I
> send you the free pack in?"

You still walk away with their email + market + a Monday touchpoint.
