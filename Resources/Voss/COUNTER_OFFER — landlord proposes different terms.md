# COUNTER_OFFER — landlord proposes different terms

**Triggers:** "I'll do it for 6 months instead of 12," "Only if you pay 3 months upfront," "I want a personal guarantee."

**Subtext:** They are negotiating, which means they are in. The terms they propose tell you what they actually fear.

**Response template:**
> Six months instead of twelve?
>
> It sounds like you want to test this without locking in for a full year in case it goes sideways. That is a reasonable place to be.
>
> Here is my challenge — at 6 months, I cannot recover the furnishing and onboarding cost on {{address}}, which runs me roughly the equivalent of two months of rent before I take a single guest. How am I supposed to make that pencil?
>
> What I can offer: full 12-month lease with a 60-day out clause that you control after month 4. You get the optionality of a short lease, I get the runway to make the unit work. Does that get us closer?
