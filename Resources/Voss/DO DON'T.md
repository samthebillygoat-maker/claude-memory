# DO / DON'T

**DON'T:**
- Don't say "I understand." It is the most distrusted phrase in negotiation. They will assume you don't.
- Don't say "to be honest with you" or "honestly" — implies everything else was a lie.
- Don't say "trust me."
- Don't lead with the rent number. Defer until they have qualified the property to you.
- Don't use the word "Airbnb" before the words "professional" or "fully-insured."
- Don't apologize for cold outreach. Confidence sells.
- Don't send a third follow-up after silence. Two is the cap on the same thread.
- Don't make round-number offers. $2,139 anchors harder than $2,100.

**DO:**
- Do say "It sounds like..." / "It seems like..." / "It looks like..."
- Do mirror their last 1-3 words as a question, then shut up.
- Do front-load their objections before they raise them.
- Do ask "How am I supposed to do that?" when they push hard on price.
- Do ask "What would need to be true for this to work for you?" to surface real objections.
- Do write short. Two sentence paragraphs. White space sells.
- Do sign off with a no-oriented question that invites opt-out.
