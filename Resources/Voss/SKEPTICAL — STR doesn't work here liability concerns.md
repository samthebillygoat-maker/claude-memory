# SKEPTICAL — STR doesn't work here / liability concerns

**Triggers:** "Airbnb is illegal here," "My HOA won't allow it," "What about liability?", "I've heard horror stories."

**Subtext:** They have read one bad article or had one bad neighbor. They want you to prove you are not that guy.

**Response template:**
> Horror stories?
>
> It sounds like you have either been burned before or watched somebody close to you get burned. That is a completely fair place to be starting from.
>
> A few things you are probably thinking that I want to address head-on: that the city will shut it down, that your insurance won't cover it, and that the neighbors will revolt. I operate on mid-term stays (30+ nights), which sit under a different regulatory bucket than nightly Airbnb in most {{city}} jurisdictions. I carry a $2M commercial liability policy with the property owner named as additional insured. And I screen every guest with ID + employer verification.
>
> What would need to be true about the operator for you to be open to a conversation?
