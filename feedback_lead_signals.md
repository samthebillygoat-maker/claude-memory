# Lead-scoring corrections

## "Short-term lease type" = bad signal, not good

If a listing advertises "short-term lease" / "flexible terms" / "month-to-month welcome," the landlord wants **flexibility**, which means they will not sign a 24-month arbitrage lease.

**What to look for instead:** "12+ months", "1 year minimum", "long-term tenant preferred."

**Why:** Sam corrected me when I flagged 6507 Bayshore as a top call based on the short-term-lease signal. The opposite is true for arbitrage — the lease length the landlord wants is the lease length you're competing against.

**How to apply:** in any lead-scoring rubric, "short-term" / "flexible" language is a **demerit**, not a bonus.

## Tampa lead-gen: apartments, not single-family homes

For Tampa specifically: target **small condos, boutique buildings, mom-and-pop multi-family.** NOT single-family homes. NOT corporate operators (Camden, AMLI, ZRS, Greystar).

**Why:** Sam said directly — "i want you to find apartments not homes." Corporate landlords reject sublet/STR clauses outright; mom-and-pop owners on small buildings will negotiate.

**How to apply:** filter SFH out of any Tampa lead pull. Filter the four corporate operators out by name. Keep buildings under ~50 units.
